STEVE JOBS

1995-2011
"Don't let the noise of others' opinions drown out your own inner voice."

BIOGRAPHY

1955: Steve Jobs was born on February 24 in San Francisco, California, and was adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs. He grew up in Silicon Valley, fostering an early interest in electronics and technology.

1976: Jobs co-founded Apple Inc. with Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne in his parents' garage. They introduced the Apple I, a pioneering personal computer that set the stage for future innovations.

1984: Apple launched the Macintosh, the first commercially successful personal computer with a graphical user interface and a mouse. Its famous Super Bowl commercial directed by Ridley Scott became a cultural landmark.

1985: Following internal power struggles at Apple, Jobs resigned from the company. He went on to establish NeXT Inc., which developed advanced computer systems aimed at the higher education and business markets.

1986: Jobs acquired The Graphics Group from Lucasfilm, which he later renamed Pixar. Under his leadership, Pixar became a powerhouse in computer animation, producing blockbuster films such as "Toy Story."

1996: Apple purchased NeXT for $427 million, bringing Jobs back to the company he co-founded. This acquisition was pivotal as NeXT's technology became the foundation for Apple's future operating systems.

2007: Apple launched the iPhone, combining a mobile phone, an iPod, and an internet communication device into one product. The iPhone set new standards for smartphones and led to the proliferation of mobile apps.

2010: Jobs introduced the iPad, a tablet computer that created a new category of mobile devices. The iPad's success reinforced Apple's reputation for innovation and design excellence.

2011: Steve Jobs resigned as CEO of Apple in August due to health issues but remained involved as Chairman of the Board. He passed away on October 5, 2011, at the age of 56, leaving behind a legacy of transformative products and a company renowned for its commitment to innovation and design.
